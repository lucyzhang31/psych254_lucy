Debriefing: In this study, you were asked to complete anagram word puzzles, given failure feedback, then asked to think of someone (e.g., yourself or your mother), and finally asked to complete more anagram word puzzles. There was some deception used in this study so that the aim would not affect your responses. We wanted to investigate how thinking of oneself or one's mother may motivate participants following failure feedback, and whether there are cultural differences in motivation following failure. The failure feedback you received was not real, and your anagram puzzles were not scored at any time in this study. I want to remind you that your data is completely confidential and that your responses will help us learn more about motivation in response to failure feedback. Please do not reveal the nature of this study at any time, as we are still collecting responses from other Mechanical Turk participants. Thank you!
